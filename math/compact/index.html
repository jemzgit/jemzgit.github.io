Compactness

Compactness is roughly the topological equivalence of finiteness in topological spaces.

Let $(X,\sigma)$ and $(X,\rho)$ be metric spaces. A function $f : X \to Y$ is continuous at a point $x \in X$ if for each $\epsilon >0$, there is a $\delta > 0$ such that if $\sigma(x,y) < \delta$ then $\rho(f(x), f(y)) < \epsilon$ .

A collection $\mathbb{U}$ of open sets is an open cover of $X$ if its union is $X$.

Let $A$ be a set in metric space $X$. An open cover is a collection $\{G_{\alpha}\}$ of open subsets of $X$ such that $A \subseteq \cup_{\alpha} G_{\alpha}$

A subset $A$ of metric space $X$ is compact if every open cover of $A$ contains a finite subcover.
